Publishing standards

Editorial policy

The standards used to keep Memory Magnets focused, original and useful to people.

StandardsPeople firstAccuracyOriginalityCommercial contentUpdates

People-first purpose

Every page should answer a real question for someone choosing, making, giving or displaying a memory magnet. We do not create near-duplicate pages for keyword variants. Search terms help us understand reader language, but they do not determine the quality or length of the answer.

Accuracy and safe instructions

Material claims and process steps should be specific enough to verify. Instructions separate essential steps from optional style choices. Safety notes are included when cutting tools, adhesives, magnets, appliances or sensitive personal images require care. Readers should always follow the manufacturer instructions supplied with their own materials and equipment.

Original writing and visuals

Articles are written for this site and illustrations are created for the subject they accompany. We do not rewrite another publisher’s article, copy product descriptions or use photographs without an appropriate right to do so. Quotations and external facts, if added, should be limited, attributed and linked to their primary source.

Commercial independence

The current site does not sell products and does not accept paid placement. If affiliate links, sponsorships or free review samples are introduced, the relationship will be disclosed near the relevant content. Payment will not guarantee a favorable assessment.

Dates, updates and corrections

Article pages show a publication or update date. A date changes only when the content receives a meaningful review or revision. Spelling corrections alone do not justify presenting an article as newly updated. Material errors should be corrected promptly and transparently.

Scope

Memory Magnets is an independent informational resource and is not affiliated with Memory Magnets Co. or another magnet seller. The site does not make ranking guarantees, medical claims or claims about a product that has not been evaluated.
